📖 How to use this page — 3 steps
1 Pick one pain point that you face this week
→
2 Copy the prompt next to it

3 Paste it into Copilot and try it once — takes under 2 minutes
💡 No training needed. Just describe what you need — like you're talking to a smart assistant.
📧 Email overload
Too many emails to read?

"Summarize this email thread and tell me what action is needed from me."

📝 After meetings
Left a meeting with messy notes?

"Summarize these notes and list the key decisions and action items with owners."

📄 Blank page problem
Need to write a report or update?

"Draft a weekly status update based on these points: [paste your notes]."

🎯 Meeting prep
Walking into a tough meeting?

"I have a meeting about [topic]. What questions should I be prepared for?"

✍️ Tricky emails
Not sure how to word something?

"Rewrite this email to sound more professional / direct / friendly: [paste email]."

📑 Long documents
Got a report you need to digest fast?

"Explain this document in simple terms and flag anything I should act on."

🔧
Pro Tip — Be specific, get better results

Instead of "write an update" → try "write a 5-line update for my VP on the Q2 budget review, keeping the tone confident." The more context you give, the better Copilot performs.

Live Example
See it in action — Email overload scenario
1 The Situation

It's Monday morning. You have 47 unread emails. You spot a long thread from Friday titled "Re: Re: Re: Q3 Budget Review – Action Required" with 12 replies. You have a leadership call in 20 minutes.

↓
2 What you type into Copilot

"Summarize this email thread and tell me what action is needed from me."

↓
3 What Copilot gives you (in seconds)
Copilot Response

Summary: The team is debating a 15% cut to the Q3 marketing budget. Finance wants a decision by EOD Monday. Sarah has proposed two options and is waiting for your approval.

Action needed from you: Review Sarah's two options (attached in her 3rd reply) and reply with your preferred choice before 5 PM today.

↓
4 The result

✅ You walked into your leadership call fully briefed in under 60 seconds — instead of scrambling through 12 emails.

Working with Documents
How to upload a file and get answers from it
Where to upload your document
📘 Word
Open the doc → click the Copilot icon on the right panel → chat with your document directly
💬 Teams / Outlook
Attach the file → then type your prompt below it, just like messaging a colleague
🤖 Copilot Chat
Go to copilot.microsoft.com → click the paperclip → upload PDF, Word, or PowerPoint
📄 Any Document
"Summarize this document in 5 bullet points and highlight what I need to decide."
🔄 Workflow / Process Doc
"This document has a process flow. Explain each step simply and flag any gaps or risks."
🖼️ Doc with Images or Diagrams
"The document contains a diagram showing [brief description]. Based on the full document, explain what this process means for my team."
📊 Report with Data
"What are the 3 most important takeaways from this report? What action should I take?"
📋 SOP / Policy Doc
"Turn this SOP into a simple checklist my team can follow."
🆚 Comparing Two Docs
"I've uploaded two versions of this document. What has changed? Summarize the key differences."
💡 Tip for docs with images: Copilot can read flowcharts and diagrams but may not always see every image clearly. If it misses something, just describe the image briefly in your prompt — "The diagram shows a 4-step approval process..." — and Copilot will factor it in.

Set Your Expectations Right
Where Copilot works well — and where it doesn't
✅ Copilot is great at...
📝
Summarising — Long emails, threads, reports, documents
✍️
Drafting — First versions of emails, updates, announcements
📋
Extracting actions — Pulling tasks, owners, deadlines from text
🔄
Reformatting — Changing tone, length, or structure of content
❓
Preparing questions — Anticipating what others might ask
📑
Creating checklists — Turning documents into step-by-step guides
❌ Don't rely on Copilot for...
🧠
Final decisions — It gives options, not answers. You decide.
🤝
People judgment — Reading the room, team dynamics, culture
🔒
Undocumented context — It only knows what's in the conversation
⚠️
Sensitive HR matters — Always involve HR for formal cases
📊
Real-time data — It doesn't pull live systems or databases
✔️
Self-verification — Always review before sending or sharing
🛡️ Responsible Use — Quick Reminders
👤
You are the decision-maker. Copilot assists — it never replaces your judgment.
👁️
Always review before sending. Treat every output as a first draft, not a final one.
🔐
Mind what you share. Don't paste confidential data or personal information into prompts.
📣
Be transparent. If Copilot helped write something you're sharing, that's fine — just know what's in it.
